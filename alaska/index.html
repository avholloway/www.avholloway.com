Pacific Northwest & Alaska Adventure

June 2026 • Seattle • Olympic National Park • Cruise

Exploring Seattle

Our journey began in Seattle. We spent our time exploring the iconic waterfront, wandering through the bustling energy of Pike Place Market, and catching some incredible views of the city skyline.

Gum Wall & Underground

We participated in the tradition of sticking our chewed gum to the walls at the market. We also took a historic tour of the Seattle underground—the original ground level of the city before the second level was built over it.

Vancouver

This is the city/port where we boarded our ship, headed to Alaska.

Koningsdam

For this cruise, we chose the Holland America Koningsdam, which provided us with stunning views, like this sunset.

Sailing the Inside Passage

Then, we set sail for Alaska. Navigating through the massive, glassy fjords, seeing tidewater glaciers for the first time, and spotting local wildlife right from the deck made for an incredible experience on the water.

Dawes Glacier

The scale of this massive glacier cannot be captured by an iPhone camera, from over a mile away. This thing is roughly the height of a 20-story building back there.

Food & Drink

We definitely made some stops for local Alaskan Food and Drink; not that we ate this specific crab, but holding it was quite the experience.

Skagway

We had three total stops: Juneau, Skagway and Ketchikan. Each port presented us with beautiful views of the land, like in this photo at Skagway.

Olympic National Park

A short side trip brought us to the breathtaking landscapes of the Olympic Peninsula. From the moss-draped, deep greens of the Hoh Rain Forest to the rugged, driftwood-lined Pacific coastline, the sheer diversity of the wilderness was unforgettable.

Forests

Multiple hikes through the park provided us with amazing views of the dense forest, large trees, and awesome canopies.

Beach

Finishing out our vacation with exploration along the beaches, like Ruby Beach, was a nice relaxing way to wind down our journey.
